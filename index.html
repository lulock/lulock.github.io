The Beginning

BlenderGuru on YouTube taught me most of the fundamentals. Like all my fellow Blender n00bs, one of my first 3D renders was a donut! I tried to challenge myself by modelling a different kind of mug - this Green Lantern mug in particular was my very favourite.

Fast forward a few years - with Blender 2.8 released, BlenderGuru re-invented his beginner series, and I followed along to brush up on my rusty skills. I didn't get as far in the tutorial before moving onto another project, but I'd like to drop my semi-complete donut below, just for a comparison!

Particle Systems

Hair / fur in Blender is fiddly but such a joy. I always want to create magic with particle systems.


The major concepts I learned doing these projects were:

  • Particle Systems
  • Weight Painting

Lighting

Enter my discovery of environment/HDR maps and pretty much lighting in general. Lighting changes everything. The more I learn about lighting, the more I am inclined to go back and fiddle with positioning light sources throughout older projects - it can honestly transform a scene.

HDR and Environment Mapping

Environment maps are not something I play around with too much, but my crash-course in this foundational element transformed my understanding of rendering scenes.


The major concepts I learned doing these projects were:

  • HDRI Environment Maps
  • Lighting
  • Materials

Low-Poly Orthographic Scenes

Today, I am loving learning from Polygon Runway. I find low-poly scenes fun and flexible. I like not being constrained by real-world dimesions and laws of physics. There's room for interpretation, exaggeration, and stylistic flair.


The major concepts I learned doing these projects were:

  • Modelling
  • Eeevee Lighting

Shaders and Materials

I really like exploring different styles. This stop-motion shader and animation style was introduced to me by Southern Shotty. Taking his cool introductory course on Skillshare helped me get over my fear of illustration and challenged me to create my own quirky character. I settled for a trailblazing ram, with pink highlighted fleece.


The major concepts I learned doing these projects were:

  • Modelling
  • Texture Painting
  • Procedural Shaders
  • Post-rendering / Compositing

The End?

Not even close. I'm always fiddling around with new projects and aspiring to learn new concepts. Below, I've listed some of my Blender heros. These resources have been crucial to helping me get to where I am today, and I've still got a long road ahead of me yet.
